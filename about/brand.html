03 — Voice & Positioning

BRAND STORY

Color Story
Cobalt and silver with an emerald edge. The silver references the rhino directly. It's not a neutral, it's a deliberate callback to the animal. Cobalt grounds it in the technical and professional. Emerald provides the energy of automation: the color of a successful script run, a green checkmark, work that got done.
Positioning
Not a dev shop. Not a vendor. A practitioner who builds automation and teaches others to participate; regardless of technical background. The rhino signals power and resilience; the automation framing signals pragmatism. Built for MSPs and IT teams who want to move faster without hiring a dev team.

05 — Logo

LOGO

The mark is a hexagonal badge built on a public-domain white-rhino silhouette (PhyloPic, CC0). The full badge carries the name plate; the compact mark drops it below 64px, where the plate text stops being legible.

Full badge

Hero, social cards, slides, stickers. Minimum size 96px.

Compact mark

Nav, favicon, avatars, any use below 64px. Minimum size 16px.

  • Dark backgrounds are primary. The badge is designed for the site's dark surfaces; a report-specific ink variant exists for the white assessment PDF only.
  • Clear space: keep at least half the hexagon's width clear on all sides.
  • Don't recolor, rotate, outline, or separate the rhino from its frame.

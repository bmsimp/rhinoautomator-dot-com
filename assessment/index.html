The Archetypes of Automation Framework

This framework helps teams identify their collective strengths and gaps across 8 dimensions of automation capability — regardless of industry, size, or technical maturity.

The core insight: the biggest barrier to automation is not technical skill — it is process thinking, team composition, and role awareness. No single person covers all eight archetypes. Teams assess collectively and use the gap analysis to guide hiring, training, or partnership decisions.

Like a sports roster, each team member brings a unique combination of strengths. The radar chart visualization makes it easy to see where coverage is strong and where critical gaps exist. Two people scoring 9 in Building but zero in Quality is a risk profile — and one this framework is designed to surface.

The 8 Archetypes

#ArchetypeWhat It Covers
1Process & Knowledge HoldingUnderstanding, retaining, and communicating how work actually happens
2Problem DefinitionIdentifying, articulating, and prioritizing what is worth automating
3Design & Logic ThinkingTranslating real-world processes into explicit logic before touching any tool
4Communication & TranslationBridging the gap between process owners and builders
5Building & ImplementationConstructing automations using tools, AI, or both
6Quality & OversightVerifying automations work correctly, including failure scenarios
7Human & Organizational AwarenessManaging adoption, resistance, ownership, and compliance
8Strategic & Vision ThinkingConnecting automation work to measurable business outcomes

Each archetype contains 3–5 named sub-archetypes — specific roles or mindsets grounded in established frameworks. Expand any archetype in the Individual Assessment tab to explore its sub-archetypes and calibrate your score.

How to Use This Tool

This web-based assessment tool lets you score individuals and teams across the 8 Archetypes of Automation. It runs entirely in your browser — no data is sent to any server.

Individual Assessment

  1. Open the Individual Assessment tab.
  2. Enter the name and role of the person being assessed (e.g. “Jane Smith, Operations Lead”).
  3. Score their capability 0–10 on each archetype. Expand each section to read the sub-archetype descriptions and ask: is this person demonstrating these characteristics?
  4. Review the live radar chart and total score as you go — they update instantly.
  5. Click + Add to Team Assessment to include this person in the team view.
  6. Repeat for each team member.

Saving & Importing Individual Results

  • Click Export to save the current assessment as a .json file you can return to later.
  • Click Import to load a previously exported .json file, then click + Add to Team Assessment to include them in the team view.

Team Assessment

  1. Collect individual assessments from each team member.
  2. Each person completes their own assessment on the Individual Assessment tab and clicks + Add to Team Assessment, or share exported .json files with a facilitator who imports them.
  3. Switch to the Team Assessment tab to see all evaluators overlaid on a single radar chart.
  4. Review the Gap Analysis card — it ranks archetypes from lowest to highest average score.

Interpreting Team Results

  • Where radars diverge on an archetype, that disagreement is worth discussing — it may indicate unclear expectations or hidden strengths.
  • Where all evaluators score an archetype low, that is your most urgent gap to address through hiring, training, or partnerships.
  • The archetype with the lowest combined team score represents your most urgent investment area.

Saving & Importing Team Results

  • Click Export on the Team Assessment tab to save the full team dataset as a .json file.
  • Click Import to reload a previously exported team assessment.

Generating a PDF Report

  1. Click Generate Report on the Team Assessment tab.
  2. Choose whether to include individual breakdowns (each person’s scores, radar chart, and per-dimension narrative on separate pages).
  3. Choose explainer depth: Short (2–3 sentence narrative per dimension) or Long (full sub-archetype breakdown with framework citations).
  4. Click Generate Report to produce a printable PDF of the full team capability profile.

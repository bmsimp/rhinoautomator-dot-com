How to Use This Tool

This web-based assessment tool lets you score individuals and teams across the 8 Archetypes of Automation. It runs entirely in your browser — no data is sent to any server.

Adding Assessments

  1. Click + Add Assessment to launch the assessment wizard.
  2. Enter the name and role of the person being assessed (e.g. “Jane Smith, Operations Lead”).
  3. Step through each of the 8 archetypes using Next and Back. Score their capability 0–10 on each one, using the sub-archetype descriptions to calibrate.
  4. On the final step, click Add to Team to include this person in the team view.
  5. Repeat for each team member.

Reviewing Results

  1. The Perspectives Radar overlays all evaluators on a single chart.
  2. Review the Gap Analysis card — it ranks archetypes from lowest to highest average score.
  3. You can also Import previously exported .json files to add assessments.

Interpreting Team Results

  • Where radars diverge on an archetype, that disagreement is worth discussing — it may indicate unclear expectations or hidden strengths.
  • Where all evaluators score an archetype low, that is your most urgent gap to address through hiring, training, or partnerships.
  • The archetype with the lowest combined team score represents your most urgent investment area.

Saving & Importing Team Results

  • Click Export on the Team Assessment tab to save the full team dataset as a .json file.
  • Click Import to reload a previously exported team assessment.

Generating a PDF Report

  1. Click Generate Report on the Team Assessment tab.
  2. Choose whether to include individual breakdowns (each person’s scores, radar chart, and per-dimension narrative on separate pages).
  3. Choose explainer depth: Short (2–3 sentence narrative per dimension) or Long (full sub-archetype breakdown with framework citations).
  4. Click Generate Report to produce a printable PDF of the full team capability profile.

Scoring Bands

Each archetype is scored on a 0–10 scale. Use these bands to calibrate:

ScoreMeaning
0–2No demonstrated capability in this area
3–4Emerging — shows instinct but no consistent, structured practice
5–6Functional — can contribute meaningfully, but with notable gaps
7–8Strong — reliable and practiced; others lean on them here
9–10Exceptional — a defining strength; sets the standard
